Gender:

During indexing, sometimes the gender of an individual could not be clearly distinguished on the original document.

The forename or abbreviated forename might be ambiguous, for example, Marion could be male or female and the entry might simply note 'lawful child of' rather than 'son or daughter of.'

In such cases, 'U' for Unstated was entered in the gender column to ensure that the wrong gender was not attributed. Selecting 'Both' will cover any entries where the gender of the child was recorded as unstated or recorded incorrectly.

Enter a range of years. You can narrow or widen the year range on the results page.

Please note that Church of Scotland and Other Church Registers end in 1855.

If you know the name(s) of the parent(s), enter these to refine your search.

The Parent Name fields can be populated by any name of either parent. For example you can enter the surname of one parent in the first field and only a forename of the other parent in the second field and only matches containing those parent names will be returned.

Please note that these indexes generally contains a father's name and a mother's name, but not always.

Please also note that some Catholic parish registers record forenames in Latin.

The indexes available on this site include Latin to English translation and you need only enter the forename in English. Your search results will return the English name followed by the Latin equivalent in brackets ‘()’ where this occurs.
