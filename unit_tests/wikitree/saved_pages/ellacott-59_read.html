Biography

Elizabeth was born in 1808 in Plymouth, Devon, England.

Elizabeth was baptised on 8 January 1808 in Plymouth, Devon, England. Plymouth, Charles the Martyr Found on findmypast[1]

Elizabeth was married on 7 October 1829 in Maker, Cornwall, England. Maker was technically part of Devon until 1844.[2]

In the 1841 census Elizabeth was living in East Stonehouse, Devon, England.[3]

In the 1851 census Elizabeth was living in Plymouth St Andrew, Devon, England. Relation to Head of House: Wife.[4]

In the 1861 census Elizabeth was living in St Pancras, Middlesex, England. Relationship: Mother.[5]

In the 1871 census Elizabeth was living in St Pancras, London, England. Relationship: Wife.[6]

In the 1881 census Elizabeth was living in Camberwell, London, England. Marital Status: Widow. Relationship to Head: Mother.[7]

Elizabeth died on 2 September 1885 in Camberwell, London. Exact date from gravestone.[8]

Elizabeth was buried on 8 September 1885 in Camden, London, England. Grave is at St. Pancras and Islington Cemetery.[9]

Research Notes

There was a family story that suggested that Elizabeth was the daughter of an Admiral. It seems unlikely that she was the daughter of Edward Ellicot since he had another daughter named Elizabeth in 1838.
