Biography

Amelia was born on 1 September 1849 in Camden, Middlesex, England. She was the daughter of John Littlemore and Mary (Dew) Littlemore.[1][2][3][4]

Amelia was baptised on 14 June 1850 in Old Church, Saint Pancras, London, England.[5][6]

In the 1851 census Amelia (age 1) was living at 45 Ferdinand St. in St. Pancras, Middlesex, England with her parents.[7]

In the 1861 census Amelia (age 11) was living in St Pancras, Middlesex, England.

When Amelia was 21 she married Harry Alfred Pavey in October 1870 in Pancras, London.[8][9][10]

In the 1871 census Amelia (age 21) was living at 65 Prince of Wales Rd, St Pancras, London, England with her husband her and 1 month old son Harry Alfred. They were sharing the house with her husband's family.[11]

In the 1881 census Amelia (age 31) was living at 367 Kentish Town Road, St Pancras, London, England with her husband, three children and one servant.[4]

Harry Alfred Pavey and family in 1881

In the 1891 census Amelia (age 41) was living in St Pancras, London, England.

In the 1901 census Amelia (age about 51) was living in St Pancras, London, England.[12]

On the census on 2 April 1911 Amelia (age 61) was living in Islington, London, England.

Pavey family holiday in Wales in 1933. Amelia with her son Arthur Eli and his wife and daughter Dorothy plus Amelia's granddaughter Dorrie.

Amelia died at the age of 88 on 16 March 1938 in Leek, Staffordshire, England. Date from gravestone.[13][14][15]

Amelia was buried on 21 March 1938 in the same family plot as her husband and his parents in Plot 1, Camden Area in St. Pancras and Islington Cemetery (this is actually in East Finchley, about 5 miles from where they lived).[15]
